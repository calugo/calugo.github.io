I’m a theoretical physicist working on mathematical and computational models of evolution.

I got my degree from U.N.A.M. (México) and my PhD from the University of Manchester under the supervision of prof. Alan J. McKane.

Since then, I have held research posts at:

  • Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (Barcelona, Spain), studying Calcium cycling’s role in cardiac dynamics within the group of Prof. Blas Echeberria

  • Centro de Astrobiología, CSIC-INTA (Madrid, Spain), studying properties of genotype-phenotype mappinngs and neutral evolution for the case of RNA, as part of the group of Prof. Susanna Manrubia

Since 2014 I’m based at The Sainsbury Laboratory, studying evolution and co-evoultion of host-pathogen systems in plants, within the Bioinformatics team lead by Dr. Dan Maclean.
